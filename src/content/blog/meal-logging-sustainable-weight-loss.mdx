## The short answer

Across decades of studies, one habit shows up again and again as a predictor of weight-loss success: writing down what you eat. Researchers call it self-monitoring, and it works less because of the numbers and more because of the awareness it creates.

## Why logging works when dieting fails

Restrictive diets ask you to follow someone else's rules. Self-monitoring does the opposite: it hands the information back to you. When you can see your own patterns, the mid-afternoon slump that becomes a vending-machine run, the dinners that arrive too late and too large, you can adjust them without white-knuckling your way through a plan you did not design.

## Logging without the math

The point is not to turn every meal into a spreadsheet. A photo, a quick note, a mental check-in: these capture the value without the burden. That is the approach this app is built around. You log in seconds, and the coaching layer turns those entries into guidance that actually fits your life.

## What sustainable looks like

Sustainable change is not a perfect week. It is a mostly-consistent month, then another. Self-monitoring is the quiet engine underneath that, because it keeps you connected to your own behavior long enough for small changes to compound.
